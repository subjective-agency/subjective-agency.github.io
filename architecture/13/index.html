    ∞ ADR-13. Media Segments Primer

    Parent to ADR-6: Transcripts, ADR-7: Media Episodes Interface, ADR-8: Transcript Mark-Up

    Context

    The domain of producing media is extremely versatile, with a number of major platforms available for our users of interest, each of which has its own specific peculiarities and requires a custom approach. Here is a non-exhaustive list of specific challenges:

    • A media episode could be published to arbitrary number of media platforms, and to arbitrary number of targets (channels), and not just by its producer but by anybody;
    • A media episode could be published to the same platform any number of times, and with non-matching attributes;
    • Apart from the content itself, a media episode may not have any identifying attributes at all;
    • A media episode may contain other episodes or fragments thereof;
    • While there is a certain pattern to the reposting dynamics, there is a significant element of random there, too;
    • Some media segments are not explicitly defined by their producers. Some media segments are tied to the personalities of their producers rather than an organization.

    These things and others make it difficult to achieve the following:

    • Create a comprehensive roster of media segments;
    • Create a mechanism for attributing a media episode to a media segment;
    • Create a mechanism for matching media episodes to identify cross-platform duplicates/re-posts and join them into media clusters.

    Entities

    Media Platforms

    Media Platform

    Media platform is an online service that allows a subset of its users to publish media items such as videos or audios for public access.

    In the database, platforms are represented with enums.media_platforms table.

    Media Targets

    Media target

    Media target, in the context of the system, is a generic term for a platform-dependent account entity, such as channel on YouTube, RuTube etc., or brand on Smotrim.ru.

    In the database, targets are represented with platform-specific tables, such as youtube_channels, smotrim_brands or rutube_channels.

    Media Episodes

    Media episode

    Media episode is a specific media item, a video or an audio, published on a media platform, under a media target, for public access.

    In the database, media episodes are collected into platform-specific tables, such as youtube_vids, smotrim_episodes, komso_episodes, or rutube_vids.

    Media Segments

    Platforms, targets and episodes are organized into similarly structured hierarchies.

    Media segment

    Media segment is a distinct series of media episodes that could be published across multiple platforms and targets while retaining sequence within the series.

    Since numerous platforms and targets could be involved, the implementation should provide a layer of abstraction on top of it to allow cross-platform matching. In the database, media segments are collected in media_segments table, and connect to the platform hierarchies on the episode level via segment_id attribute.

    Media Episodes Clusters

    Media Episode Cluster

    Media episode cluster is a set that contains media episodes that were published on different platforms on different occasions but are the same episode in essence.

    Clusters are a different layer of abstraction required to account for duplicates across platforms and targets without dismissing them. We don't want to dismiss them because, in the context of the project, non-technical duplicates (i.e. those that occur in the result of a unintended technical error) still work to increase the propaganda coverage. In the database, clusters of media episodes are collected into the media_episodes_clusters table, and connect to the platform hierarchies on the episode level via cluster_id attribute.

    When first added, a media episode is not assigned to any cluster (i.e. has cluster_id = NULL).
